Of Night and Ruin

I did not expect the house to remain intact. Time rarely grants abandoned places such restraint...

The silence did not feel empty. Only patient.

Someone seated exactly where I now stood. Waiting.

The age grows restless beyond tolerance.

Light encroaches where darkness once ruled unchallenged. Survival now favors absence over presence.

Therefore I withdraw.

Let the centuries turn without my witness.

This place shall remain sufficient.

When quiet returns to the world, I may walk within it once more.

Until then, memory sleeps.

I became aware of the room again only gradually. The house stood empty. Yet the final line lingered long after I closed the book.

Until then.
